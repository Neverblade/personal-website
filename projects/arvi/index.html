Augmented Reality for the Visually Impaired

James Lin, Mengshi Feng – Dec. 2018


Augmented Reality for the Visually Impaired (ARVI) is an iOS app intended to give the blind a way to locate important spatial objects through the combined use of the phone camera and spatial audio cues. Finding particular objects in an open environment has traditionally been an inconvenient task for the visually impaired, and ARVI was designed to combat that.

The core idea is that people would use our app to scan environments and drop audio cues at important landmarks (bus stops, doorways, etc.), then upload that data to the cloud. A blind user could then later pull down that information and hear the audio cues, allowing them to navigate to it through sound.

This work was primarily built off of the technologies of SLAM (simultaneous localization and mapping) and binaural audio. We developed ARVI for UC Berkeley’s CS294: Virtual Reality and Immersive Computing.
